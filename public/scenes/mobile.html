Outer Courtyard

Medieval castles were built during the Middle Ages in Europe and the Middle East to provide protection for royalty from invaders.

During the medieval times the great hall was the centrepiece of the castle, and where everyone dined together.

The kitchen was built next to the great hall so that food could be delivered quickly to the king and members of the royal family before it got cold.

Almost all the food was grown or caught on the king's estate, and it was kept in a storeroom called the pantry. Meat and fish were the central dishes and people ate food with their fingers off bread plates called trenchers.

Medieval weapons were used by both Medieval Knights and their their foot soldiers and archers support. The common most weapons included swords, maces, sprears, and longbows.

The castle guards were considered the gatekeepers of the castle, and were responsible for guarding different castle gates so that enemies could not get into the castle.

Castles housed any number of servants who served the royal family. From priests and entertainers, to cooks and weavers, castles provided work for many different people.

Cats and dogs were kept in castles to help kill rats and keep them from eating the grain stores.

Often times attacking armies would wait outside and try to starve out the people living in the castle instead of attacking them. This is called a siege. Many castles were built on a spring so they would have water during a siege.

Originally towers were built with square tops, but later were replaced by round towers that offered better defense and visibility.

The knights were heavily armored and well trained soldiers who rode on horseback. Only the wealthiest nobles could afford to be a knight. They needed very expensive armor, weapons, and a powerful war horse.

The word 'castle' is derived from the Latin word 'castrum' meaning 'Fortified place'.

The sleeping chambers was for sleeping and private quarters and used by the kings's family. It also became a private sitting room favoured by the family.

The room in the castle called the Great Chamber, was a large bedroom and used by the king and queen of the castle.

In the throne room is where the king would look over official ceremonies, hold council, grant audiences, receive honor, award high honors, and perform other official functions.
